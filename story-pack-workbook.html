Module 01 · Before → After Transformation Story
Build Your Core Reinvention Narrative

Create a clear, intentional narrative that explains your transition without apology or oversharing. This is your master story — everything else in this pack builds from it.

1
Context — Where You Were
2–3 sentences. Background, not biography.
NO LONG TIMELINES · NO EMOTIONAL LANGUAGE

"For the last ___ years, I was focused on ___, primarily working on ___."

2
Catalyst — What Shifted
The moment or realization that changed your direction.
NO BLAME · NO DRAMA · NO VICTIM LANGUAGE

"Over time, I realized ___."   or   "A key moment for me was ___."

3
Choice — Your Intentional Decision
Show agency — your pivot was a decision, not a reaction.
SIGNAL CONTROL + MATURITY

"I decided to ___ so that ___."

4
Capability — Your Value Now
Translate past experience into present, specific value.
SPECIFIC + OUTCOME-FOCUSED

"What I bring now is ___."

5
Direction — Where You're Going
End with forward-facing clarity — no hedging. One clear sentence.
ONE CLEAR SENTENCE

"I'm now focused on ___."

Final Story Assembly — Combine all five into one narrative (30–45 seconds when spoken)

Module 02 · Tell Me About Yourself Script
Answer the Most Common Question Without Rambling

5 sentences. 30 seconds. Forward-facing. Write three versions of the same core story — one for each context. You're done when you can deliver each version out loud without notes in under 30 seconds.

The Script Structure
"I started in ___, where I focused on ___.
Over time, I realized ___.
I decided to ___.
Today, I help ___ by ___.
I'm currently looking for ___."
Version 1 — Interview
Most formal · role-specific · high stakes

Tailor to the specific role. Name the company or industry. Close with what you're looking for that matches the position.

Version 2 — Networking
Warmer tone · conversation-starter · no job ask

Replace the job ask with genuine curiosity. End with a question to the other person — "I'm exploring X — what's been your experience in that space?"

Version 3 — Casual / Social
Relaxed · shorter · no jargon

For when someone asks "What do you do?" at a dinner, event, or cold introduction. 2–3 sentences max. No titles. No companies. Just the work and where you're going.

Module 04 · Networking & Intro Scripts
Sound Confident and Intentional in Every Conversation

Your networking script is shorter and warmer than your interview script — but it still needs to be intentional. Use the right version for the right context, and always end with curiosity rather than a direct ask.

Script A — Short Intro
10–15 seconds · Cold introductions, name badges, quick asks

"I work with ___, helping them ___."

Script B — Medium Intro
20–30 seconds · Networking events, warm intros, "tell me what you're up to" conversations

"I spent ___ years in ___, and now I focus on ___ because ___."

Script C — Pivot Intro
20–25 seconds · When someone already knows your background and asks about the change

"I recently shifted toward ___, building on my experience in ___. What I'm finding is ___."

My Follow-Up Lines — Prepare 2–3 for when they ask more

Module 05 · Transition Talking Points
Handle Tough Questions With Confidence

Sensitive questions are not obstacles — they're opportunities to reframe and redirect. The rule: never defend. Reframe and redirect. Use these fill-in frames to prepare for every scenario before it happens.

"Why did you leave?"

"That experience taught me ___. It showed me I'm strongest when ___. That's why I'm now focused on ___."

"Why the change?"

"Over time, I realized ___ — and I decided to ___. This wasn't reactive; it was intentional."

"What happened there?"

"It was a ___ environment. I learned ___ and then decided to move toward ___ instead."

"Is this a step back?"

"I see it as a deliberate move toward ___. What I bring is ___ — which is directly what this role needs."
